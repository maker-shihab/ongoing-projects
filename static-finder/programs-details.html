The "Forschungszulage" is a German government funding program designed to financially support companies of all sizes in their research and development activities, enhancing their capacity for innovation.

Funding Objectives

  • Boost innovation in companies
  • Foster research-driven growth
  • Enhance technological competitiveness
  • Secure Germany's position as a leading innovator

Introduction

Unlike traditional grants, this program operates as a refundable tax credit, reimbursing companies up to 25% of eligible personnel costs related to their R&D efforts, capped at €1 million per fiscal year.

A key differentiator of this initiative is its broad accessibility; it targets companies of all sizes, from startups to large corporations, ensuring comprehensive support for innovation. The eligibility criteria are straightforward and apply to various fields, including technological innovations, product improvements, and experimental development.

Furthermore, the Forschungszulage stands out due to its predictability and ease of application, providing companies with clarity and reduced administrative burden. This allows businesses to plan their R&D investments confidently, contributing significantly to Germany's long-term competitiveness and economic growth.

Who is funded

The Forschungszulage is designed to support a wide range of companies engaged in research and development activities. Eligible recipients include businesses of all sizes—from innovative startups and small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs) to large corporations—across various industries and technological sectors operating within Germany.
