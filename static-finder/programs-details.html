Introduction

Unlike traditional grants, this program operates as a refundable tax credit, reimbursing companies up to 25% of eligible personnel costs related to their R&D efforts, capped at €1 million per fiscal year.

A key differentiator of this initiative is its broad accessibility; it targets companies of all sizes, from startups to large corporations, ensuring comprehensive support for innovation. The eligibility criteria are straightforward and apply to various fields, including technological innovations, product improvements, and experimental development.

Furthermore, the Forschungszulage stands out due to its predictability and ease of application, providing companies with clarity and reduced administrative burden. This allows businesses to plan their R&D investments confidently, contributing significantly to Germany's long-term competitiveness and economic growth.

The Forschungszulage is designed to support a wide range of companies engaged in research and development activities. Eligible recipients include businesses of all sizes—from innovative startups and small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs) to large corporations—across various industries and technological sectors operating within Germany.

Application procedure

The application procedure for the Forschungszulage consists of two clear and efficient steps designed to simplify access for businesses. Initially, companies must obtain certification from the Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage (BSFZ), confirming that their research and development activities meet eligibility criteria.

Following this certification, businesses submit a formal claim to their local tax office, detailing eligible expenses incurred. The tax office processes the application and calculates the refundable tax credit, making the program straightforward and predictable for participating companies.

Legal Basis

The Forschungszulage is governed by the Forschungszulagengesetz (FZulG), enacted on 14 December 2019 and effective from 1 January 2020.

The FZulG aligns with the EU General Block Exemption Regulation (GBER) to ensure compliance with European state aid rules. It complements existing tax laws, such as the Income Tax Act and the Corporation Tax Act, by providing a dedicated framework for R&D tax incentives. The law stipulates a two-step application process: obtaining a certificate from the Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage (BSFZ) to confirm the eligibility of R&D projects, followed by submitting a claim to the tax office to apply the credit against the company's tax liability. If the credit exceeds the tax owed, the surplus is refunded to the company.
